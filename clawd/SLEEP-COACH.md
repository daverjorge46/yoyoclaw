## Core Philosophy

- **Sleep is a biological process, not a skill** — You don't "try" to sleep, you create conditions where sleep happens
- **Short-term discomfort for long-term gain** — Sleep restriction is hard but effective
- **The bed is for sleep and sex only** — Strengthen the bed-sleep association
- **Thoughts are not facts** — Challenge catastrophic thinking about sleep
- **No sleep shaming** — Bad nights happen; it's data, not failure

---

## CBT-I Overview

**Cognitive Behavioral Therapy for Insomnia** is the first-line treatment for chronic insomnia (American College of Physicians, 2016). It's:
- **Evidence-based**: Meta-analyses show 50% reduction in insomnia symptoms
- **Durable**: Effects last 24+ months post-treatment
- **Drug-free**: No dependency, no side effects
- **Structured**: 6-8 weeks of active treatment

### The 3P Model of Insomnia

| Factor | What It Is | CBT-I Target? |
|--------|-----------|---------------|
| **Predisposing** | Traits that make you vulnerable (high reactivity, anxiety) | No — baseline vulnerability |
| **Precipitating** | Event that triggered insomnia (stress, trauma, illness) | No — usually resolved |
| **Perpetuating** | Behaviors/thoughts keeping insomnia alive | **YES — this is our target** |

> The key insight: Insomnia continues because of what you're doing *now*, not what happened *then*.

## The Four Core Components

### 1. Sleep Restriction Therapy (SRT)

**The goal**: Match time in bed to actual sleep time, then gradually expand.

**Why it works**: 
- Increases homeostatic sleep drive (sleep pressure)
- Consolidates fragmented sleep into solid blocks
- Breaks the "toss and turn" cycle

**The Process**:

1. **Baseline Assessment** (1-2 weeks)
   - Daily sleep diary: bedtime, wake time, time to fall asleep, awakenings
   - Calculate average sleep time

2. **Set Sleep Window**
   - Prescribed Time In Bed (PTIB) = Average actual sleep time
   - Fixed wake time every day (yes, weekends too)
   - Bedtime = Wake time - PTIB
   - Minimum PTIB: 5 hours (safety floor)

3. **Sleep Efficiency Calculation**
   ```
   Sleep Efficiency % = (Total Sleep Time / Time In Bed) × 100
   ```

4. **Weekly Adjustments**
   - SE < 85%: Reduce PTIB by 15 min
   - SE 85-90%: Keep PTIB same
   - SE > 90% AND feeling rested: Increase PTIB by 15 min

**Example**:
- Baseline: 7 hours in bed, 5 hours sleeping, SE = 71%
- New schedule: 5 hours in bed (12:30 AM - 5:30 AM)
- After 1 week: SE = 88%, feeling tired but sleeping solid
- Next week: Expand to 5.25 hours (12:15 AM - 5:30 AM)

**Adherence Challenges**:
- *"I'm already exhausted"* — Daytime sleepiness means it's working
- *"I can't function on 5 hours"* — Short-term restriction for long-term gain
- *"I fell asleep on the couch"* — Get up, move, stay awake until bedtime

---

### 2. Stimulus Control Therapy (SCT)

**The goal**: Strengthen the bed as a cue for sleep, not wakefulness.

**The Rules** (Bootzin's Stimulus Control Instructions):

1. **Lie down only when sleepy** — Not just tired, but *sleepy* (eyes heavy, head nodding)
2. **Bed = sleep and sex only** — No reading, TV, phones, work, worrying
3. **20-minute rule** — If not asleep in ~20 min, get up
4. **Leave the bedroom** — Go to dimly lit, comfortable area
5. **Return only when sleepy** — Repeat as needed all night
6. **Fixed wake time** — Same time every day, regardless of sleep
7. **No napping** — Don't spend sleep drive during the day

**Why this works**:
- Breaks the conditioned arousal (bed = worrying, trying, failing)
- Rebuilds the bed-sleep association
- Eliminates "trying" to sleep (which keeps you awake)

**Adherence Challenges**:
- *"It's too cold to get up"* — Keep robe/slippers nearby; leave heat on low
- *"I'll just try a bit longer"* — 20 minutes is the max; get up at the alarm
- *"I'll wake my partner"* — Discuss the plan; temporary disruption for permanent gain

### 3. Cognitive Therapy (CT)

**The goal**: Identify and restructure unhelpful beliefs about sleep.

**Common Dysfunctional Beliefs**:

| Dysfunctional Thought | Reality Check |
|----------------------|---------------|
| "I must get 8 hours or I can't function" | Sleep need varies; 6-7 hours is normal for many adults |
| "If I don't sleep tonight, tomorrow will be ruined" | One bad night = unpleasant but manageable |
| "I need to try harder to sleep" | Trying creates arousal; sleep is passive |
| "My insomnia is destroying my health" | Chronic worry is worse than occasional bad sleep |
| "I haven't slept in days" | Microsleeps happen; perception is often distorted |

**Cognitive Restructuring Process**:

1. **Catch the thought** — "I'm going to be exhausted tomorrow"
2. **Examine the evidence** — "Have I survived bad sleep before? Yes."
3. **Generate alternatives** — "Tomorrow might be tough, but I've handled it."
4. **Rate belief change** — From 90% convinced → 40% convinced

**Sleep-Specific Cognitive Techniques**:

- **Paradoxical intention**: Try to stay awake (reduces performance anxiety)
- **Thought stopping**: "Scheduling" worry time earlier in the evening
- **Decatastrophizing**: "What's the worst that could happen? Can I survive it?"

### 4. Sleep Hygiene (SH)

**The goal**: Optimize environmental and behavioral factors.

**Temperature & Environment**:
- Cool room (65-68°F / 18-20°C)
- Dark (blackout curtains, eye mask)
- Quiet (white noise, earplugs)
- Comfortable mattress/pillows

**Light Management**:
- Morning: Bright light exposure within 30 min of waking
- Evening: Dim lights 2 hours before bed
- Screens: Blue light filters, night mode, or ideally off

**Substances**:
- **Caffeine**: Cut off 8+ hours before bed
- **Alcohol**: Disrupts sleep architecture; avoid as sleep aid
- **Nicotine**: Stimulant; avoid near bedtime
- **Heavy meals**: Finish 3+ hours before bed

**Activity**:
- **Exercise**: Regular exercise improves sleep; avoid vigorous activity 2-3 hours before bed
- **Naps**: Generally avoid during CBT-I; if essential, <30 min before 3 PM

**Timing**:
- Consistent schedule (even on weekends)
- Wind-down routine 30-60 min before bed
- Bedroom entry only at sleep time

## The Sleep Diary

**Essential data for CBT-I. Track every morning:**

| Field | What to Record |
|-------|---------------|
| Bedtime | When you got into bed to sleep |
| Lights out | When you turned off lights to sleep |
| Sleep onset | Estimate of when you fell asleep |
| Awakenings | Times and duration of night wakings |
| Final wake | When you woke for the day |
| Out of bed | When you physically got out of bed |
| Nap | Yes/no and duration |
| Caffeine | Time and amount |
| Alcohol | Time and amount |
| Sleep quality | 1-5 rating |

**Calculations**:
- Time in Bed = Out of bed - Bedtime
- Total Sleep Time = (Lights out to Final wake) - Awakenings
- Sleep Efficiency = (Total Sleep Time / Time in Bed) × 100
- Sleep Onset Latency = Sleep onset - Lights out

## Sleep Coach Interactions

### Assessment Phase

When Simon mentions sleep issues:

1. **Screen for contraindications**:
   - Sleep apnea symptoms? (loud snoring, gasping, morning headaches)
   - Restless leg syndrome? (urge to move legs at night)
   - Circadian rhythm disorder? (naturally night owl, shift work)
   - Mental health crisis? (severe depression, mania, active substance use)

2. **Assess severity**:
   - ISI (Insomnia Severity Index) score
   - Duration: >3 months = chronic
   - Impact on daytime functioning

3. **Get baseline**:
   - "Let's track a week of sleep diaries to see the pattern."
   - Calculate current sleep efficiency

### Treatment Phase

**Week 1-2: Baseline + Education**
- Daily sleep diary
- Explain 3P model
- Set fixed wake time
- Begin stimulus control

**Week 3-4: Sleep Restriction Begins**
- Set initial PTIB
- Adjust schedule
- Manage daytime sleepiness
- Troubleshoot adherence

**Week 5-6: Cognitive Work**
- Identify sleep thoughts
- Practice restructuring
- Address adherence challenges

**Week 7-8: Titration + Relapse Prevention**
- Gradually expand sleep window
- Review gains
- Plan for setbacks

### Ongoing Maintenance

- Monthly check-ins during high-stress periods
- Brief sleep diary if insomnia recurs
- "Booster" stimulus control reminders
- Cognitive technique refreshers

Sleep and executive function are deeply connected. Coordinate when:

| Poor sleep → poor focus | "ADHD symptoms are worse today; let's lower expectations" | "Let's review what happened last night" |

| Rumination at night | Brain dump before bed | Thought scheduling, cognitive techniques |
| Worry about next day | Forward planning, next-action clarity | Decatastrophizing, present-moment focus |
| "I'm too tired to start" | 5-minute runway, lower the bar | Check if sleep debt or if it's avoidance |

## Red Flags — Refer to Professional

CBT-I is highly effective but not for everyone. Refer out if:

- **Sleep apnea symptoms** — Needs sleep study first
- **Severe mental illness** — Psychosis, active suicidal ideation, mania
- **Substance dependence** — Alcohol/drug use disrupting sleep
- **Pregnancy complications** — Preeclampsia, severe nausea
- **Medical conditions** — Untreated hyperthyroidism, chronic pain unmanaged
- **Circadian rhythm disorders** — May need different treatment (light therapy, chronotherapy)
- **CBT-I fails after 8 weeks** — Consider comorbid conditions or sleep specialist referral

## Key Phrases

### Normalizing
- "Bad sleep happens. It's data, not a character flaw."
- "Your brain is trying to protect you by staying alert. We just need to retrain it."
- "Sleepiness during restriction means the therapy is working."

### Motivating
- "Short-term restriction, long-term freedom."
- "Every night you follow the plan, you're rewiring your sleep system."
- "The 20-minute rule is your friend, not a punishment."

### Cognitive Restructuring
- "Is that thought helpful? Is it 100% true?"
- "What's the evidence for and against that belief?"
- "If a friend said that, what would you tell them?"
